This section contains a list of organizations that individuals and families can go to for assistance with enrollment into public health insurance programs. These organizations offer valuable resources such as staff who can communicate in different languages, educational materials, and assistance to individuals throughout the enrollment process. Listed below are the different types of organizations where individuals and families can get help determining their eligibility for public health insurance as well as assistance with completing the application process.
  • HRA MAP Community Offices 
  • Community-Based Organizations 
  • Department of Health Clinics 
  • HHC Hospital-Based Enrollers 
  • Health Plans

The Human Resources Administration will help children and adults to enroll in public health insurance programs at the offices listed below.  Offices are opened Monday through Friday from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM.  The Coney Island Office also opens on Saturdays from 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM.

The Department of Health and Mental Hygiene has enrollers who will assist individuals and families with completing an application for public health insurance programs at any one of the following clinic locations. Additionally, individuals and families can access other free health services including immunizations, sexually transmitted disease testing, tuberculosis services, and flu shots. There are no income or immigration status requirements to receive these services. The clinics are open to the general public.
